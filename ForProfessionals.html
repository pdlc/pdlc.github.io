What is a Neuropsychologist?

  • A Neuropsychologist is a psychologist with advanced training and expertise in how behaviour and skills are related to brain structures and systems
  • Assessment and treatment of children with acquired and developmental disorders

What is a Neuropsychology Assessment?

  • Assessment of a range of cognitive skills including – intellectual abilities, memory, learning, attention/concentration, executive functions (e.g. problem solving, planning/organisation, impulsivity etc), visual-spatial skills, processing speed and academic achievement
  • Assessment of behaviour, emotional functioning and social skills
  • Determine the impact of a child’s cognitive skills on academic performance
  • Establish a child’s learning profile (i.e. pattern of strengths and weaknesses)
  • To assist in diagnosis, educational placement, remediation, clinical treatment planning

What service does PDLC offer?

Diagnostic Evaluation:

  • Neuropsychology assessment accompanied by a clinical interview
  • Achievement assessment (e.g. literacy and numeracy)
  • Assessment of behaviour, emotional functioning and social skills

Treatment and Follow-up Support:

  • Comprehensive report summarising results
  • Verbal feedback of results to parents and teachers
  • Individualised (a) cognitive remediation strategies customised to each child’s needs, and (b) recommendations to modifications to the child’s curriculum School/IEP consultation

When to refer for a Neuropsychology Assessment?

  • A child is aged 6 years to 16 years 11 months
  • A child has a known (or suspected) developmental or acquired condition that is commonly associated with cognitive and learning difficulties (e.g. ADHD, dyslexia, brain injury)
  • A child has learning or behavioural difficulties at school
  • A child has been assessed by a school psychologist and the IQ assessment raised questions about cognitive functioning that required further investigation
  • A child has had a previous cognitive assessment. Since children’s skills go through periods of rapid change, and they are faced with increasing cognitive and academic demands with each passing year, there is often a need to conduct periodic re-assessments.

What is the duration of an Assessment?

An assessment will take approximately four hours with breaks provided as needed
