What is a Neuropsychologist?

A neuropsychologist is a specialist psychologist who is interested in a child’s academic performance, their cognitive/thinking skills (such as concentration, memory, problem solving, planning skills, and speed of thinking), and their social and emotional development.

How long will it take?

Assessment usually takes about 4 hours and is generally conducted over 1 or 2 sessions. A feedback session will also be scheduled with you to discuss your child’s results.

What is offered?

  • A neuropsychology assessment will provide an understanding of your child’s unique cognitive (i.e. thinking) skills and how this may impact on their academic performance. What they are good at and what they may need some help with—and why!
  • Recommended classroom (and at-home) strategies based on your child’s unique learning profile – to help your child to maximise their academic potential.
  • Consultation with your child’s teacher to recommend classroom strategies, adjustments to their curriculum & provide input into the IEP (individual education plan) process.
  • A report showing your child’s strengths & weaknesses in learning...and how to help.

What will my child do?

Your child will be asked to answer questions as well as complete paper and pencil tasks.

What should I tell my child about the assessment?

You can tell your child they are going to see a “talking doctor” who will ask questions, play games and have them draw pictures. It is important that your child knows there will be no needles or anything scary.
